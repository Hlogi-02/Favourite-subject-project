Natural Disasters
A natural disaster is characterized by the abnormal intensity of a natural agent (flood, mudslide, earthquake, avalanche, drought) when the usual measures to be taken to prevent this damage were not able to prevent their emergence or were not able to be taken.
Favourite School Subject: Geography
Geography is the scientific study of Earth's surface, its physical features, and human populations, focusing on where things are, why they are there, and how they change over time. It is an interdisciplinary field that examines both the natural environment, such as landforms and climate, and the human environment, including culture, politics, and settlements.
Rural, Suburban And Urban
Urban, suburban, and rural describe types of communities: Urban areas are dense city centers with high populations and tall buildings; suburban areas are residential areas near cities, offering a mix of homes and businesses with lower density than urban areas; and rural areas are characterized by low population density, much open space, and a landscape of farms and natural scenery
Map coordinates
Map coordinates define a precise location on Earth using latitude and longitude. Latitude measures a point's north or south distance from the Equator, while longitude measures its east or west distance from the Prime Meridian
Types Of Landforms
Landforms are the natural physical features on Earth's surface, shaped by processes like tectonic forces, erosion, and volcanic activity, including mountains (elevated land with steep slopes), valleys (low areas between mountains), plateaus (flat-topped highlands), plains (vast flat areas), deserts (arid regions), and rivers and coasts (shaped by water).
Types Of Rivers
Rivers are diverse, categorized by their water flow as permanent (year-round), periodic (seasonal), episodic (rarely, after heavy rain), and exotic (flowing from a wet area into a dry one). Geographically, rivers are analyzed by their longitudinal profile (source to mouth), cross-profile (a cross-section view), and their distinct upper, middle, and lower courses, each with unique landforms like waterfalls, meanders, and floodplains.
